A simple guide to the Geological Survey of India's 25-year plan[cite: 1, 2].

The Vision (Our Dream) [cite: 41]

[cite_start]

To become a world-class center of science that uses earth knowledge to make India wealthy, safe from natural disasters, and green[cite: 42].

[cite_start]

The Mission (Our Job) [cite: 43]

[cite_start]

To map every inch of India's land, find the minerals we need for the future, predict landslides or floods, and share all this data with the public[cite: 44].

[cite_start]

The 7 Big Goals [cite: 50]

1. [cite_start]Finding the Building Blocks of Tomorrow [cite: 118]

[cite_start]

What it is: Finding rare minerals like Lithium and Cobalt deep under the ground or under the sea[cite: 119, 120].

[cite_start]

Example: Finding Lithium in India so we can make our own batteries for electric cars and smartphones instead of buying them from other countries[cite: 35, 121].

2. [cite_start]Using Smart Technology & Robots [cite: 125]

[cite_start]

What it is: Replacing old hammers and maps with Artificial Intelligence (AI), drones, and high-tech sensors[cite: 126, 127].

[cite_start]

Example: Using a drone with a "mineral-detecting camera" to scan a mountain that is too steep for humans to climb[cite: 130].

3. [cite_start]Keeping People Safe [cite: 132]

[cite_start]

What it is: Predicting landslides and floods before they happen to save lives and homes[cite: 133, 134].

[cite_start]

Example: Setting up an "Early Warning System" in a Himalayan village that sends an alert to people's phones 24 hours before a landslide might happen[cite: 135, 138, 141].

4. [cite_start]Learning from the World [cite: 142]

[cite_start]

What it is: Working with top scientists from countries like the US, Australia, and Canada to share secrets and tools[cite: 143, 147].

[cite_start]

Example: Indian and Australian scientists working together to map the deep ocean floor using the best tech from both nations[cite: 147].

5. [cite_start]Open Data for Everyone [cite: 149]

[cite_start]

What it is: Taking millions of old paper maps and putting them online so anyone—students or business owners—can use them for free[cite: 151, 152].

[cite_start]

Example: A college student downloading a free 3D map of their hometown to study why certain areas have better groundwater than others[cite: 153, 168].

6. [cite_start]Teaching the Next Generation [cite: 169]

[cite_start]

What it is: Training GSI staff and students to become "Earth experts" using Virtual Reality and new degree programs[cite: 172, 174, 176].

[cite_start]

Example: Using a VR headset to "walk" through a volcanic area to learn about rocks without having to travel thousands of miles[cite: 176].

7. [cite_start]Helping Government Make Rules [cite: 180]

[cite_start]

What it is: Giving the government scientific proof so they can make better laws about mining and the environment[cite: 181, 182].

[cite_start]

Example: GSI showing a map of "safe areas" to the government so they know exactly where it is safe to build a new highway[cite: 183, 184].
