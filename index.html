Vision 2047 – Geological Survey of India

Strategic Roadmap for National Leadership in Geoscience

Introduction

The Geological Survey of India plays a foundational role in national development by providing critical geoscientific inputs for infrastructure, mineral security, disaster preparedness, environmental stewardship, and strategic planning. It supports energy security, self-reliance, and sustainable development through modern technologies such as AI, remote sensing, airborne geophysics, and isotopic analysis.

Vision and Mission (2047)

Vision: To develop into a world-class, innovative, and agile multidisciplinary geoscience institution empowering India with objective geological knowledge for economic prosperity, environmental resilience, and sustainable socio-economic development.

Mission: To systematically map India’s geology, discover and quantify mineral and energy resources (except oil and gas), assess geohazards, address climate challenges, and disseminate geospatial data openly for informed decision-making.

Strategic Pillar 1: Economic Geology & Critical Mineral Resource Mapping

Mandate and Scope

Systematic identification, mapping, and evaluation of critical and strategic minerals essential for energy transition, technological advancement, and national security. Includes on-shore and off-shore exploration, mineral system mapping, and 3D/4D mapping.

Short Term Actions (0–5 Years) 0–5
  • Complete NGPM
  • Pegmatite Mapping of India
  • Critical mineral exploration (onshore and offshore)
  • NAGMP Phase II
  • EM surveys in mineralised belts
  • Coal, lignite, shale gas, geothermal, hydrogen exploration
  • REE extraction from fly ash
Medium Term Actions (0–10 Years) 0–10
  • NAGMP Phase III
  • Predictive geological mapping
  • Deep stratigraphic drilling
  • National mineral system mapping
Long Term Actions (0–15 Years) 0–15
  • 3D/4D maps of five cratons
  • Subsurface critical mineral mapping
  • IndMAP pan-India MT imaging
Vision 2047 Outcomes 2047
  • 3D/4D geological map of India
  • Complete national geochronology and isotope mapping
  • Identification of all critical mineral prospects
