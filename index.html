Vision 2047 – Geological Survey of India

Strategic Roadmap for National Leadership in Geoscience

Introduction

The Geological Survey of India plays a foundational role in national development by providing geoscientific inputs for infrastructure, mineral security, disaster preparedness, environmental stewardship, and strategic planning. GSI supports energy security, self-reliance, and sustainable development through advanced technologies such as AI, remote sensing, airborne geophysics, and isotopic analysis.

Vision and Mission for 2047

Vision: To develop into a world-class, innovative, and agile multidisciplinary geoscience institution empowering India with objective geological knowledge for economic prosperity, environmental resilience, and sustainable socio-economic development.

Mission: To systematically map India’s geological framework; discover and quantify mineral and energy resources except oil and gas; assess geohazards and climate change challenges; and disseminate geospatial data openly for informed decision-making.

1. Economic Geology & Critical Mineral Resource Mapping

Scope and Intent

Systematic identification, mapping, and evaluation of critical and strategic minerals essential for energy transition, technological advancement, and national security through onshore and offshore exploration and 3D/4D mapping.

Short Term (0–5 Years) 0–5
  • Complete NGPM and NAGMP Phase II
  • Critical mineral exploration onshore and offshore
  • Pegmatite mapping and EM surveys
  • Coal, lignite, shale gas, geothermal, hydrogen exploration
  • REE extraction from fly ash
Medium Term (0–10 Years) 0–10
  • NAGMP Phase III
  • Predictive geological mapping
  • Deep stratigraphic drilling
  • National mineral system mapping
Long Term (0–15 Years) 0–15
  • 3D/4D maps of five cratons
  • Subsurface critical mineral mapping
  • IndMAP pan-India MT imaging
Vision 2047 Outcomes 2047
  • 3D/4D geological map of India
  • National geochronology and isotope mapping
  • Identification of all critical mineral prospects
